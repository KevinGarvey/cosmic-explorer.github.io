Guidance for Land Acknowledgement  

The Land Acknowledgement is an invitation to self-educate about the cultures, ecosystems, and histories that shape our experiences of, interactions with, and responsibility to the spaces we are in as well as the people who have called that place home for time immemorial. Every member of the Cosmic Explorer community has the responsibility to authentically participate in cultivating a culture that is based on respect, reciprocity, and allyship with the Indigenous stewards of the world.

 

The Land Acknowledgement may be read verbatim during a presentation, or it can be displayed while you (the presenter) speak about what this statement means to you and its relationship to the ways that you practice science. You may choose to recognize by name the Indigenous people who are vitally connected to the land as a first step in this process. You may consider how the science you practice benefits from Indigenous displacement and make a statement about your commitment to change. You may ask the audience to join you in learning more about Land Back, MMIW/MMIWG/MMIP, and other political actions important within Indigenous Communities. You may consider providing resources for further education or ways to become involved. You may also share stories in a respectful manner. At any time, please feel free to reach out to the CE Office of Land & Community Partnerships for support.



Land Acknowledgement  

The Cosmic Explorer team recognizes the inherent connections between the lands, waters, sky, and people. We are committed to cultivating connections to place and partnerships with Indigenous communities who care for, have cared for, and will continue to care for these places. We acknowledge that we are responsible for the manner in which we do science and for the impacts it has on the land and its Peoples.
